Cristian V.A. Munteanu, PhD
Researcher in the Bioinformatics and Structural Biochemistry Group,
Institute of Biochemistry of the Romanian Academy
 
  		 
 
ResearcherID: I-9290-2016
Position: Coordinator of IBAR mass spectrometry facility
Expertise: Analytical and preparative HPLC, nano HPLC, high-resolution mass spectrometry (MS) and tandem MS, computational mass spectrometry (statistical analysis of proteomics data and R scripts or functions development for MS data analysis), sample preparation for MS based proteomics analysis, basic biochemistry and cell culture methods
Interests: Differential proteomics: investigation of protein-protein interactions in the degradation of misfolded proteins from endoplasmic reticulum in melanoma and their role in MHC class I antigen presentation with a special focus on tyrosinase and tyrosinase related proteins
Discovery proteomics and peptidomics: identification of MHC class I presented epitopes in melanoma and their mechanism of production at the molecular level
Glycoproteomics: investigation of O- and N-linked glycopeptides and glycoproteins with site-specific characterization of the occupancy and glycan heterogeneity
Thesis Title: Insights into functional interaction proteomics of endoplasmic reticulum associated degradation (ERAD) and antigen presentation in melanoma using mass spectrometry [Abstract]
